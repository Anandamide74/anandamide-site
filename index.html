Framework · Original Research

Consciousness-Based Human
Performance Paradigm

"Athato Brahma Jijnasa" — Now, therefore, the inquiry into the nature of consciousness. — Brahma Sutras of Badarayana, c. 500 BCE

The CBHPP is an original framework developed by Pradeep Pillay that proposes a consciousness-inclusive approach to self-regulation, wellbeing, and performance. It integrates the ancient Indian understanding of Brain, Mind, and Awareness with contemporary neuroscience, contemplative science, and applied performance methodology.

Where neuroscience studies the survival brain, CBHPP asks: what becomes possible when the mind is brought under conscious direction? The difference between surviving and living — that is the domain of this inquiry.

Request the Full Paper
01
Brain — Instrument of Survival
The survival apparatus — reactive, externally-oriented, threat-focused. Neuroscience studies this well. CBHPP starts here and goes further.
02
Mind — The Outward-Turning Organ
The subtle organ that converts survival signals into narrative and identity. The mechanism that keeps the human being turned toward the external world.
03
Awareness — Prior to Both
Pure awareness — the witness that knows both brain states and mind activity. The field within which all experience arises. The starting point of the inward journey.
→
Surviving vs Living
Surviving: operating from the brain's fear-driven survival apparatus. Living: what becomes possible when the mind comes under conscious direction. This distinction is the heart of CBHPP — and of Anandamide's methodology.
