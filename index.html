The CBHPP Core Philosophy
We Train Brain, Mind,
and Consciousness

Every Anandamide programme is grounded in the Consciousness-Based Human Performance Paradigm (CBHPP) — a framework that proposes that the most underinvestigated determinant of human performance is the regulatory condition of the conscious observer.

Modern sports science has developed extraordinary tools for training the brain and body. CBHPP extends this — systematically training the mind to regulate its outward pull, and ultimately opening the door to consciousness development: the recognition of pure awareness as the ground of the human being.

Athato Brahma Jijnasa
"Now, therefore, the inquiry into the nature of consciousness."
— Brahma Sutras of Badarayana (c.500 BCE)

The Vedic Rishis, Gautama Buddha, Swami Vivekananda, and the Indian philosophical tradition understood what modern science is only beginning to investigate — that the journey inward is the highest form of human development.

Educated in the United Kingdom and Ireland, he has spent 25+ years asking the question mainstream sports science rarely asks — what is the condition of the human being inside the performance?

His CBHPP framework — the Consciousness-Based Human Performance Paradigm — has been published on PhilPapers and submitted to Prof. David Chalmers at NYU, the world's leading philosopher of consciousness. An international two-cohort pilot study is currently in design, based in Ahmedabad and open to participants globally.

His teaching philosophy is simple: we train not only the body and brain, but the mind and consciousness. Because real performance — in sport, in life, in leadership — comes from the inside out.
